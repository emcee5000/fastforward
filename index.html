Fast Forward  

If you have an idea, you can make it happen at Syracuse University.

The University wants to help fund your idea. Students are our greatest asset, and we want to see their ideas come to life. Chancellor Kent Syverud has allocated grants to fund student project ideas.

This video contest will allow you to express your idea for showcasing an element of work at Syracuse University that shows how a great, private institution like Syracuse and its students can have a positive impact on the world.

How it works

In a short video, you will need to convey why your idea is important and relevant to the University community, and how you would execute your idea. Examples of things that can be funded include, but are not limited to: symposiums, performances, installations, research experiments, new technologies, and works of art.

Your project should celebrate the past, illuminate the present, and  Fast Forward  into the future. Preference will be given to projects that have the most benefit for the greater University. p to 12 projects will be selected, and will receive a $1000 grant from the University.

The Basics

  1. Videos can be no longer than 3 minutes.
  2. Videos must be uploaded to YouTube, Vimeo, or a similar public video hosting service.
  3. All participants must be students currently enrolled at Syracuse University
  4. The project proposed via your video submission needs to be feasibly funded within the grant budget of $1000.00
  5. Winning teams will get a chance to pitch your idea at Chancellor Syverud's inauguration.
  6. If selected for a grant, your team will be expected to the project by December 31, 2014.
