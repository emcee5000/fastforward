Fast Forward  

If you have an idea, you can make it happen at Syracuse University.

The University wants to help fund your idea. Students are our greatest asset, and we want to see your ideas come to life. As part of Chancellor Kent Syverud's inauguration on April 11th, the University has allocated grants to fund student projects. The winning project proposals will be presented live at a special event with the Chancellor on April 11th.

The Inauguration Fast Forward competition will allow you to express your idea for showcasing an element of work at Syracuse University that shows how a great, private institution like Syracuse and its students can have a positive impact on the world.

Spread the Word

How it works

The competition begins with a short video. You will need to convey why your idea is important and relevant to the University community, and how you would execute your idea.

From this video pitch, up to 12 projects will be selected, and each will receive a grant for up to $1,500 from the University to carry out the idea. Your project can celebrate the past, illuminate the present, or project into the future.

Think about what you could do for up to $1,500. How could you move the mission of the University  forward ?

The Basics

  1. Videos can be no longer than 3 minutes.
  2. Videos must be uploaded to YouTube, Vimeo, or a similar public video hosting service.
  3. You may participate on your own, or as part of a team.
  4. All participants must be students currently enrolled at Syracuse University.
  5. The project proposed via your video submission needs to be feasibly funded within the grant budget of up $1,500.
  6. Winning entrants will get a chance to pitch your idea at a special event on April 11th as part of Chancellor Syverud's inauguration.
  7. If selected for a grant, you or members of your team will be expected to complete your project by December 31, 2014.

The Fine Print

Entrants will be notified by 10:00 AM on Monday, April 7 if they have been selected as a grant recipient.
