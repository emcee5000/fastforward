Fast Forward  

If you have an idea, you can make it happen at Syracuse University.

The University wants to help fund your idea. Students are our greatest asset, and we want to see your ideas come to life. Chancellor Kent Syverud has allocated grants to fund student projects. As part of his inauguration celebration on April 11th, the award-winning project proposals will be presented live to the Chancellor at a special event.

The Chancellor’s Fast Forward competition will allow you to express your idea for showcasing an element of work at Syracuse University that shows how a great, private institution like Syracuse and its students can have a positive impact on the world.

Spread the Word

How it works

The competition begins with a short video. You will need to convey why your idea is important and relevant to the University community, and how you would execute your idea.

From this video pitch, up to 12 projects will be selected, and each will receive a grant for up to $1500 from the University to carry out the idea. Your project can celebrate the past, illuminate the present, or project into the future.

Think about what you could do for $1500. How could you move the mission of the University  forward ? How can you make an impact on the world, on the community, or on the campus?

The Basics

  1. Videos can be no longer than 3 minutes.
  2. Videos must be uploaded to YouTube, Vimeo, or a similar public video hosting service.
  3. You may participate on your own, or as part of a team.
  4. All participants must be students currently enrolled at Syracuse University
  5. The project proposed via your video submission needs to be feasibly funded within the grant budget of $1500.00
  6. Winning entrants will get a chance to pitch your idea at Chancellor Syverud's inauguration.
  7. If selected for a grant, you or members of your team will be expected to complete your project by December 31, 2014.

The Fine Print

Entrants will be notified by 10:00 AM on Monday, April 7 if they have been selected as a grant recipient. If you are selected as a recipient in the competition, at least one member of your project team must be willing to appear and pitch your idea on stage at an event that is part of Chancellor Syverud’s Inauguration on Friday, April 11. Prior to the Chancellor's Installation event, all recipients are strongly encouraged to attend pitch and public speaking training to be held at various times during the week of April 7.

Entry Form

Entry forms with video URLs must be filled out and submitted by 11:59 PM on Monday, March 31, 2014.
